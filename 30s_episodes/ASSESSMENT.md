# 30s FORMAT — ASSESSMENT & RATIONALE
_2026-06-30_

## What Changed (15s → 30s)

### The Narrator
The narrator went from a 1-line setup to THE funniest character. She now has 3+ lines per episode, commenting like a sports commentator who despises the sport. Every line is savage. She sets up jokes, undercuts drama, drops deadpan asides mid-action.

### Physical Comedy
The 15s versions had no room for actual slapstick. The 30s versions have:
- Bonnets flying in slow motion with smoke trails
- Characters being dragged by their ears across ballrooms
- Ponies slipping, cats flying through air, faceplants into puddles
- Snore bubbles, nose flicks, scone pile faceplants
- Random ducks with top hats

### Character Exaggeration
- Lady W-B: Now wears 3 stacked bonnets. Doesn't enter rooms — DETONATES into them. Foams at the mouth.
- Biscuit: Newspaper is larger than his body. Hasn't been awake since 1812. Waves from behind hedges.
- Darcy: Top hat so tall it brushes chandeliers. Pupils dilate when nose-flicked. Falls off ponies.
- Eliza: Chewed bonnet, mud boot, elite eyebrow game. Flicks Darcy on the nose. Doesn't flinch.

### Beat Structure
Every 30s episode now has a clear farce structure:
setup → escalation → complication → punchline → stinger

### Hooks & Stingers
Every episode opens with an unmissable visual hook and ends on a freeze-frame stinger with on-screen text.
This is native TikTok language — designed to make you NEED the next episode.

### On-Screen Text
The 30s format embraces freeze-frame text gags at the end of each episode.
These replace the previous "NO TEXT ON SCREEN" rule because this is a comedy format,
not a cinematic one. The text is part of the joke.

## Divergence from 15s Scripts
EP.04 and EP.05 in the 30s version skip ahead in the story:
- EP.04 becomes "The Rain Walk Incident" (Darcy's pratfall)
- EP.05 becomes "The First Proposal" (Darcy gets rejected)

The original EP.04 "Five Daughters, Zero Plans" and EP.05 "The Letter"
can be added as EP.06 and EP.07 or re-ordered in the 30s timeline.
