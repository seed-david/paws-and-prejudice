# PAWS & PREJUDICE — Episode Scripts

## STANDING RULES (apply to every episode)
- Every character is a dog or cat. No humans. Ever.
- All characters stand upright on two legs like humans (bipedal, anthropomorphic)
- CLOSE SHOT throughout — characters fill the vertical 9:16 frame
- **CLASSICAL MUSIC IS MANDATORY in every episode** — specify the exact style per scene mood:
  - Comedy/chaos: harpsichord stabs, pizzicato strings, Rossini overture energy
  - Scandal/drama: full dramatic orchestra swell, fortissimo strings
  - Romance: gentle string quartet, solo violin
  - Tension: single cello, low strings building
  - Physical comedy: bouncy allegro strings with percussive comedic stabs
  - Fainting/tragedy: swooning violin glissando
- Format: Dreamina Seedance 2.0 Fast, Omni reference, 9:16, 15s
- Send each render to Telegram for David's sign-off before posting to TikTok

## EP.01: "A Single Dog of Good Fortune"
**Scene:** Drawing room. Lady Woofington-Barks announces the arrival of an eligible bachelor.
**Characters:** Lady Woofington-Barks (Pomeranian), Lord Biscuit-Bottoms (Basset Hound)
**Music:** Lady Woofington-Barks Overture (#2) → Biscuit-Bottoms Deadpan (#1)
**Status:** NEEDS RERENDER WITH DIALOGUE

**NARRATOR VO:** "It is a truth universally acknowledged that a Pomeranian in possession of information will dispense it like a fire alarm."
LADY WOOFINGTON-BARKS: *(exploding through the door at full velocity, bonnet completely airborne, letter in both paws shaking like a treaty)* "BISCUIT. BISCUIT. BISCUIT-BOTTOMS I NEED YOU TO BE CONSCIOUS FOR THIS."
LORD BISCUIT-BOTTOMS: *(behind broadsheet, breathing)* "Mmm."
LADY WOOFINGTON-BARKS: *(climbing onto the settee for height, gripping his lapels, paws either side of his face)* "A SINGLE GENTLEMAN. FIVE THOUSAND A YEAR. TAKEN NETHERFIELD PARK. HE IS A CAT, BISCUIT, AND HE IS ABSOLUTELY DRENCHED IN ELIGIBILITY."
LORD BISCUIT-BOTTOMS: *(page turns)* "Is he."
LADY WOOFINGTON-BARKS: *(to camera, devastated, shaking)* "FIVE DAUGHTERS. FIVE. And he just — he just turns a PAGE."

## EP.02: "He Dances With NOBODY"
**Scene:** Grand Regency ballroom, chandeliers blazing, dozens of dogs in formal wear.
**Characters:** Lady Woofington-Barks, Lord Biscuit-Bottoms, Lord Darcy Pawsworth
**Music:** Full Regency Waltz (#4) into Biscuit-Bottoms Deadpan (#1) on final beat
**Status:** NEEDS RERENDER WITH DIALOGUE

LADY WOOFINGTON-BARKS: *(gripping Lord Biscuit-Bottoms' arm so hard his coat tears slightly, hissing at volume that ends all nearby conversations)* "HE. IS. HERE. The CAT in the top hat. Darcy Pawsworth. Ten thousand a year, Biscuit, TEN THOUSAND, he has not danced with a single soul and he is simply STANDING THERE being WEALTHY and I cannot STAND IT."
LORD DARCY PAWSWORTH: *(surveying the assembled company as if selecting livestock for disposal)* "...Tolerable neighbourhood. Insufferable dogs."
LADY WOOFINGTON-BARKS: *(paws clasped under chin, vibrating)* "He is MAGNIFICENT."
LORD BISCUIT-BOTTOMS: *(has fallen asleep standing up)*
LADY WOOFINGTON-BARKS: *(elbowing him)* "BISCUIT WAKE UP HE'S LOOKING."
LORD BISCUIT-BOTTOMS: *(opening one eye)* "Is he."

## EP.03: "Tolerable, I Suppose"
**Scene:** Ballroom edge. Darcy and Eliza encounter each other for the first time.
**Characters:** Lord Darcy Pawsworth, Eliza Fetchworth
**Music:** The Darcy Stare (#3) single cello note, sustained, painful
**Status:** NEEDS RERENDER WITH DIALOGUE

LORD DARCY PAWSWORTH: *(to Bingley, not even bothering to lower his voice, gesturing at Eliza with one gloved claw)* "She is handsome enough, I grant you. Though the bonnet suggests a recent altercation with a hedge. And she appears to be — yes, she is watching me say this."
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(already watching him say this, ears flat, one eyebrow raised into the stratosphere)* "I am. Yes."
LORD DARCY PAWSWORTH: *(a single blink. Adjusts cravat)* "Then you have saved me the trouble of repeating it."
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(to camera, jaw slightly open)* "...Did this cat just —"
LORD DARCY PAWSWORTH: *(already walking away)* "Good evening."
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(to camera, livid, somehow still polite)* "I have never hated anyone faster in my entire life."

## EP.04: "Five Daughters, Zero Plans"
**Scene:** Drawing room. Lady Woofington-Barks in full nervous breakdown.
**Characters:** Lady Woofington-Barks, Lord Biscuit-Bottoms
**Music:** Lady Woofington-Barks Overture (#2) escalating in pitch and speed until something breaks, collapses into Biscuit-Bottoms Deadpan (#1)
**Status:** NEEDS RERENDER WITH DIALOGUE

LADY WOOFINGTON-BARKS: *(has been pacing for what is visibly hours. Bonnet completely destroyed. One ruffle hanging off her shoulder by a thread)* "Biscuit. BISCUIT. The entail will pass to that insufferable little Chihuahua Collins the MOMENT you die — WHICH COULD BE ANY DAY — and where will I be? WHERE WILL THE GIRLS BE? In the HEDGES, Biscuit, IN THE HEDGES."
LORD BISCUIT-BOTTOMS: *(unmoved behind broadsheet)* "I am in reasonable health."
LADY WOOFINGTON-BARKS: *(spinning to face camera, paws grasping the air)* "JANE has refused to chase the cat OPENLY. LYDIA has eloped in her MIND with every officer in the county. MARY is playing the pianoforte AS WE SPEAK and NOBODY ASKED HER TO."
*(From offscreen: scales. Wrong notes. Very confident)*
LORD BISCUIT-BOTTOMS: *(turns page)* "She's improving."
LADY WOOFINGTON-BARKS: *(collapses onto the settee, paws over face)* "WE ARE RUINED."

## EP.05: "The Letter"
**Scene:** Longbourn garden. Eliza reads a letter. The letter is CATASTROPHIC.
**Characters:** Eliza Fetchworth
**Music:** The Scandal Sting (#11) — three VIOLENT pizzicato stabs — dead silence — then single cello dragging like a funeral
**Status:** NEEDS RENDER WITH DIALOGUE

**NARRATOR VO:** "Miss Eliza Fetchworth had received many letters. None had yet required her to sit down immediately in a flowerbed."
*(Eliza reads. Her ear twitches. Then her tail. Her face cycles through curiosity, confusion, dawning horror, white-hot fury)*
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(voice absolutely level, which is somehow worse)* "He. Has. GONE."
*(She reads the next line. She reads it again. She puts the letter down)*
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(to the garden, with the terrible calm of someone who has already decided several things)* "Caroline Bingley wrote this letter. With her own PAW. And described Jane as — " *(she picks it up, reads, face curdling)* "— 'not sufficiently handsome to tempt a gentleman of Mr Bingley's taste.'"
*(A very long pause. A robin lands nearby and immediately flies away)*
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(folding the letter into a very small square, jaw set)* "Jane. Does not. Know yet."
*(She straightens her bonnet. It goes crooked immediately)*
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(walking back to the house with the expression of someone preparing to be magnificent under insufferable circumstances)* "Right. Fine."
**NARRATOR VO:** "Some letters should be burned. This one would be read fourteen more times before breakfast."

## EP.06: "The Proposal (Absolutely Catastrophic)"
**Scene:** Drawing room. Darcy proposes. It is the worst proposal in recorded history.
**Characters:** Lord Darcy Pawsworth, Eliza Fetchworth
**Music:** Romance strings that immediately collapse into horror strings mid-sentence
**Status:** DRAFT — NEEDS FULL DIALOGUE

LORD DARCY PAWSWORTH: *(has rehearsed this for weeks. Paws clasped. Voice controlled — barely)* "Miss Fetchworth. I find myself — against every reasonable judgement — compelled to inform you that I have formed a violent attachment to your person. Despite your family's frankly spectacular lack of fortune, your mother's extraordinary volume, your youngest sisters' moral chaos, and your uncle being in trade —"
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(staring at him)* "Are you... proposing to me?"
LORD DARCY PAWSWORTH: "— I am willing to overlook all of this. You may consider this an honour of the very highest —"
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(standing up slowly)* "You are proposing to me. While listing my family's deficiencies."
LORD DARCY PAWSWORTH: *(realising, half a second too late)* "I — the phrasing —"
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: "NO."
*(Absolute silence)*
LORD DARCY PAWSWORTH: "...I beg your pardon?"
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: "NO. Good evening."
*(She leaves. He remains. Alone. A single petal falls from the vase)*
**NARRATOR VO:** "Lord Darcy Pawsworth had never been told no. He found he had no idea what to do with it."

## EP.07: "Mr Collins' Proposal"
**Scene:** Drawing room. Mr Collins proposes. He does not pause for breath once.
**Characters:** Mr Collins (Chihuahua), Eliza Fetchworth
**Music:** Pompous bassoon and piccolo combination, increasingly frantic
**Status:** DRAFT — NEEDS FULL DIALOGUE

MR COLLINS: *(hat in paws, puffed to absolute maximum size, having clearly rehearsed since birth)* "Miss Fetchworth. It is my HONOUR — nay, my DUTY — as a humble servant of the Church and most faithful servant of my most gracious patroness Lady Catherine de Paw whose condescension is beyond what I can EXPRESS —"
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: "Mr Collins —"
MR COLLINS: "— to inform you that I have selected you as my future companion. The honour I do you, Miss Fetchworth, is CONSIDERABLE. Lady Catherine herself expressed the view that I should marry, and I OBEY. You will not find a more sensible match. Your alternative, I fear, is poverty."
*(He beams. He is very small in a very large waistcoat)*
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(long pause)* "Mr Collins. I am honoured. And I am refusing."
MR COLLINS: *(not processing)* "Ha! Yes. The charming reluctance. Lady Catherine predicted this exactly."
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: "I am genuinely refusing."
MR COLLINS: *(still beaming)* "Of course you are."

## EP.08: "Wickham is a SCOUNDREL"
**Scene:** Assembly rooms. Wickham turns on the charm. Every dog melts. Eliza is suspicious.
**Characters:** Wickham (Siamese cat), Eliza Fetchworth, Lydia Fetchworth
**Music:** Charming salon piano — slightly too smooth, slightly too perfect
**Status:** DRAFT — NEEDS FULL DIALOGUE

WICKHAM: *(leaning against the wall with the practiced ease of a cat who has ruined approximately seven lives)* "Miss Fetchworth. What a pleasure. I've heard so much about you from — well, from everyone, really. You seem to be the only sensible creature in the county."
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(to camera, already suspicious)* "He's very charming."
WICKHAM: *(sighing beautifully)* "I shouldn't say this. But Darcy Pawsworth? We were raised as brothers. And what he did to me — " *(sad smile)* "— I shan't burden you with it. It was simply... wrong."
LYDIA FETCHWORTH: *(materialising from nowhere, elbowing Eliza)* "HE IS GORGEOUS. HIS WHISKERS. ELIZA HIS WHISKERS —"
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(to camera, quieter)* "Everything he's saying is probably a lie."
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(also to camera, quieter still)* "His whiskers really are incredible though."

## EP.09: "The Letter (The Good One)"
**Scene:** Garden. Darcy's second letter arrives. Eliza reads. Everything changes.
**Characters:** Eliza Fetchworth
**Music:** Opens with Scandal Sting, slowly transforms into something genuinely moving
**Status:** DRAFT — NEEDS FULL DIALOGUE

*(Eliza reads. And reads. And reads. Her ear does a very complicated thing)*
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(barely above a whisper)* "Wickham did what?"
*(She reads more)*
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: "He did what to Darcy's — " *(reads, jaw drops, reads more)* "...Georgiana?"
*(She puts the letter down. Picks it up. Puts it down. Picks it up)*
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(to the garden, with dawning, awful clarity)* "I have been the most complete fool. In England. Possibly in Europe."
*(She looks at the house. Then back at the letter)*
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(quietly)* "I owe that unbearable cat an apology."
*(She puts the letter in her pocket. Straightens her bonnet. It goes crooked)*
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(walking away, jaw set, soft)* "...Absolutely insufferable."
**NARRATOR VO:** "Miss Eliza Fetchworth had been wrong before. Never quite this spectacularly."

## EP.10: "The Wedding"
**Scene:** Absolute pandemonium. Everyone crying. Lady Woofington-Barks has fainted twice already.
**Characters:** All. Everyone. Full cast.
**Music:** FULL ORCHESTRA. Swelling. Triumphant. Lady W-B's screams as counterpoint.
**Status:** DRAFT — SEASON FINALE

*(The ceremony. Eliza and Darcy standing side by side)*
LORD DARCY PAWSWORTH: *(looking at Eliza sideways, quietly)* "I should tell you. I am still not entirely sure how this happened."
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(also looking forward, also quiet)* "You proposed terribly."
LORD DARCY PAWSWORTH: "And yet."
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: "And yet."
*(From the pews: Lady Woofington-Barks, face buried in a handkerchief the size of a bed sheet, weeping at maximum volume)*
LADY WOOFINGTON-BARKS: "SHE'S DONE IT. BISCUIT SHE'S ACTUALLY DONE IT. TEN THOUSAND A YEAR BISCUIT TEN THOUSAND —"
LORD BISCUIT-BOTTOMS: *(unmoved in the front pew)* "Mmm."
*(Lydia has already kicked off her shoes. Mary is playing the organ with catastrophic confidence. Kitty has fainted out of excitement and nobody has noticed)*
ELIZA FETCHWORTH: *(to Darcy, smiling sideways)* "Still tolerable?"
LORD DARCY PAWSWORTH: *(the ghost of a smile. The very ghost)* "Considerably above tolerable."
*(Lady Woofington-Barks faints directly into the wedding cake)*
**NARRATOR VO:** "It was, by any measure, the most scandalous, most catastrophic, most magnificent wedding the county had ever produced. Lady Woofington-Barks had been unconscious for three of the best parts. She did not regret a moment."
