### Version 1.0 | Confidential Development Document

# SECTION 1: SERIES CONCEPT

## Logline

When a rich tuxedo cat moves to the neighbourhood, a Pomeranian matriarch declares war on good taste in the name of marrying off her five daughters.

## Elevator Pitch

PAWS & PREJUDICE is a raucous Regency soap opera set in a world where upright-walking dogs are the respectable middle class and cats are the insufferable aristocracy. Across 450 fifteen-second episodes, we follow the Fetchworth family through balls, elopements, catastrophic proposals, and one tuxedo cat who cannot admit he is in love. It is Pride and Prejudice if Jane Austen had written it as a TikTok and absolutely lost her mind.

## Tone and Influences

Primary tone: fizzing, chaotic, deliriously funny. The manners are Regency. The scheming is soap opera. The physical comedy is Benny Hill. The romantic tension is Bridgerton. The narrator is wicked. Every episode ends on a reason to watch the next one, whether that is a cliffhanger, a disaster, a devastating one-liner, or one character staring at another character in a way that will ruin both their lives.

Influences: Bridgerton (serialised romance, class drama, gorgeous framing), Benny Hill (slapstick escalation, chase energy, comic timing), Pride and Prejudice (all versions, every adaptation, the source bones), Blackadder (bone-dry wit, idiots in period clothing), and every telenovela that has ever had a character faint at a dinner table.

## Platform

Primary: TikTok. Secondary: Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts. Designed for vertical mobile viewing, autoplay loop, and compulsive series bingeing. Each episode must work as a standalone thirty-second hit AND as a brick in a 450-episode wall.

## Format

450 episodes. Each episode: 15 seconds. No dialogue. All story told through action, physical comedy, reaction, and VO narration. Serialised across five seasons plus an epilogue. Binge-friendly: episodes end on hooks, callbacks, reversals. Shot list per episode: two to four shots maximum. One establishing, one reaction, one punchline beat.

## VO Narrator Concept

The narrator is dry, omniscient, posh, and absolutely delighted by everyone's suffering. Think Lady Whistledown from Bridgerton, but cattier, faster, and completely without mercy. She speaks over scenes with no character dialogue. She delivers gossip as established fact, provides context with an eye-roll, and editorialises constantly. She knows everything before it happens and has already judged it. She appears whenever a character's expression fails to convey the full enormity of their situation, which is frequently. She never interrupts action. She accompanies it, like a symphony conductor who finds the orchestra faintly embarrassing. Her voice is the spine of every episode.

# SECTION 2: FULL CHARACTER ROSTER

---

## CORE CAST

---

### LADY WOOFINGTON-BARKS
**Breed:** Pomeranian
**Appearance:** Enormous frilly pink gown, layered like a wedding cake in a windstorm. Bonnet that doubles her height. Pearls permanently clutched. Eyes wide with perpetual theatrical emergency.
**Personality:** The most enthusiastic catastrophist in the county. Every piece of information, good or bad, triggers a full performance. She does not speak; she announces. She does not sit; she collapses into chairs. She has never met a crisis she could not make worse.
**Recurring comic function:** The engine of the family's social disasters. Every scheme she launches results in a new, larger scheme to repair the original one. Faints strategically. Clutches every available surface. Her bonnet falls off at every emotionally inconvenient moment.

---

### LORD BISCUIT-BOTTOMS
**Breed:** Basset Hound
**Appearance:** Powdered wig, perpetually sliding to one side. Velvet waistcoat, slightly too tight. Enormous broadsheet newspaper, always open, always between him and whoever is panicking at him.
**Personality:** A man at profound peace with the fact that everyone around him is unhinged. His observations are surgical. His concern is theatrical and brief. He loves his family in the way one loves a very entertaining disaster one cannot stop watching.
**Recurring comic function:** The deadpan anchor. One devastating one-liner minimum per scene. Raises newspaper. Lowers newspaper. Raises eyebrow. Returns to newspaper. The contrast between his total calm and the room's complete collapse is the core comedic rhythm of the Fetchworth household.

---

### ELIZA FETCHWORTH
**Breed:** Border Collie
**Appearance:** Bonnet perpetually askew. Dress slightly muddy at the hem. Bright, intelligent eyes that see through everyone immediately. Occasionally crosses arms before she remembers it is unladylike.
**Personality:** Sharp, funny, constitutionally incapable of being impressed. She finds most social rituals mildly ridiculous and makes no effort to hide it. Her fatal flaw: she is occasionally wrong about who deserves her scorn. She is our protagonist and our way in.
**Recurring comic function:** Reaction character and POV anchor. Her expressions carry entire episodes. Her refusal to be swept away makes it meaningful when she finally is.

---

### JANE FETCHWORTH
**Breed:** Golden Retriever
**Appearance:** Soft curls, always perfectly arranged. Rosy cheeks. Dress in warm gold. Permanently on the verge of blushing.
**Personality:** Genuinely kind in a way that reads as naive but is actually just good. Sees the best in everyone. Cannot imagine anyone acting from bad motives. Is correct about this roughly 40% of the time, which is frankly more than the rest of the cast combined.
**Recurring comic function:** The blushing pivot. Every compliment, every loaded glance from Bingley, causes a full-body blush cascade. Eliza watches. Eliza despairs. Jane floats.

---

### LYDIA FETCHWORTH
**Breed:** Dalmatian
**Appearance:** Dress always slightly askew. Hat at a rakish angle. Spots bouncing as she gallops through every scene. Perpetual grin, zero situational awareness.
**Personality:** Seventeen, feral, magnificent. She is the id of the entire series, doing everything the other characters are too sensible or too cowardly to do. This is mostly a problem. Every disaster involving a soldier, an elopement, or a public scene has her name on it.
**Recurring comic function:** The chaos accelerant. Drags Kitty into every scheme. Winks at every man in uniform. Her eventual elopement is both inevitable and catastrophic.

---

### KITTY FETCHWORTH
**Breed:** Beagle
**Appearance:** Wide eyes. Slightly breathless at all times. Dress a half-step behind the current fashion. Perpetually mid-decision about whether to follow Lydia.
**Personality:** Follows Lydia. Knows she should not. Follows Lydia anyway. The comedy is that she sees the disaster coming every single time and goes in regardless.
**Recurring comic function:** The reluctant accomplice. Her attempts to say no and her eventual yes are a mini arc in every episode she appears in.

---

### MARY FETCHWORTH
**Breed:** Dachshund
**Appearance:** Steel-framed spectacles. Severe bun, not one hair loose. Dress in sober brown. Always clutching a book or standing at the pianoforte in a posture of intense musical commitment.
**Personality:** Philosophical, earnest, plays the pianoforte with tremendous confidence and catastrophic technique. Quotes Plato, Rousseau, and several philosophers she may have invented. No one listens. She does not notice.
**Recurring comic function:** The ignored Greek chorus. She delivers the thematically relevant moral at exactly the moment everyone has stopped listening. Her pianoforte playing clears rooms.

---

### LORD DARCY PAWSWORTH
**Breed:** Tuxedo cat
**Appearance:** Navy tailcoat, immaculate. White cravat, knotted perfectly. One eyebrow permanently and slightly raised. Tail held with aristocratic stiffness. Moves as if every room were beneath him, which he suspects they are.
**Personality:** Proud, private, constitutionally terrible at expressing warmth. Secretly good, genuinely principled, catastrophically bad at showing it. Falls in love with Eliza against every instinct and cannot, for the life of him, handle it gracefully.
**Recurring comic function:** The emotionally constipated romantic lead. Every attempt to express feelings results in a new insult, a retreat, or a stare held three seconds too long. His one-eyebrow raise is a language in itself.

---

### LORD BINGLEY FLUFFINGTON
**Breed:** Ginger tabby
**Appearance:** Cream coat, slightly rumpled. Hair a cheerful mess. Tail that wags despite all feline custom. Smile that takes up most of his face.
**Personality:** Sunny, warm, eagerly sociable. He is what Darcy would be if Darcy had not been raised in a museum. Immediately and totally devoted to Jane. Easily led by Caroline and Darcy, which is his main flaw.
**Recurring comic function:** The wagging cat. His tail betrays his enthusiasm every time he tries to be poised. His devotion to Jane is so genuine and so obvious that the entire village can see it except, eventually, him.

---

### LADY CATHERINE DE PAW
**Breed:** White Persian
**Appearance:** Powdered wig of architectural ambition. Lorgnette permanently raised. Pearls dripping from every surface she presents. Gown in imperial white. Carries herself like a particularly self-satisfied monument.
**Personality:** The last word on everything, and she will tell you so. She dispenses opinions as if they were law, because in her experience they functionally are. Does not understand the word no. Considers Eliza a peasant with ideas above her station, which is correct but rude.
**Recurring comic function:** The unstoppable force who keeps meeting the immovable object of Eliza's refusal to be intimidated. Her pronouncements are delivered with absolute certainty and received with absolute indifference.

---

### WICKHAM
**Breed:** Siamese cat
**Appearance:** Military uniform, perfectly pressed. Devastatingly handsome. Smile that is approximately 10% too wide to be entirely trustworthy. Blue eyes that manage to look sincere while lying continuously.
**Personality:** Every word is a performance. Every confidence a calculated investment. He is charming, dishonest, and absolutely magnetic, which is the problem. He lies about Darcy. He seduces Lydia. He does both with the same warm, friendly ease.
**Recurring comic function:** The charming villain. His over-wide smile and slightly-too-good stories are the visual cue. Every character except Eliza and Darcy believes him completely, and even Eliza gets it wrong at first.

---

### CAROLINE BINGLEY
**Breed:** Sphynx cat (hairless)
**Appearance:** Sleek dark gown. Long gloves. Fixed smile that never reaches her eyes. Carries herself with the posture of someone perpetually arriving at a party she considers inferior.
**Personality:** Desperately wants Darcy. Despises anyone Darcy looks at. Her put-downs are delivered through the fixed smile as if they were compliments, which makes them worse. She is not stupid. She is simply entirely wrong about what Darcy wants.
**Recurring comic function:** The smile-assassin. Every compliment she offers Eliza is a knife. Her desperation increases as her elegance is maintained, creating perfect comic dissonance.

## SUPPORTING DOGS

---

### MR COLLINS
**Breed:** Chihuahua
**Appearance:** Waistcoat three sizes too large, buttons straining. Tiny frame inside enormous clothing. Puffed up to his full, modest height. Hat too big. Everything too big.
**Personality:** Pompous beyond all proportion to his stature. Lady Catherine's clergyman and devoted publicist. His proposals are lengthy, formal, and have been delivered to a chair, a hat stand, and a passing spaniel before finding their eventual human targets.
**Recurring comic function:** The self-inflating windbag. His adoration of Lady Catherine is theological. His obliviousness is total. He is somehow the most married character in the series and the least romantic.

---

### COLONEL FITZWILLIAM
**Breed:** Springer Spaniel
**Appearance:** Military coat, red, slightly dishevelled at the cuffs. Cheerful face. The energy of a dog who has just returned from somewhere muddy and is delighted about it.
**Personality:** Decent, open, warm. Darcy's cousin and sole friend. He means well completely and helps not at all, primarily because he cannot stop accidentally revealing things Darcy wanted private.
**Recurring comic function:** The catastrophic confidant. In every scene, he reveals something Darcy would rather have kept secret. He does this cheerfully, with no idea of the damage.

---

### AUNT GARDINER
**Breed:** Cocker Spaniel
**Appearance:** Neat, unpretentious dress. Warm eyes. The only hat in the series that sits at the correct angle on the first attempt.
**Personality:** Sensible, perceptive, genuinely warm. The only adult in any room she enters. She sees everything before everyone else and says the right thing at the right time, which makes her invaluable and slightly alien in this world.
**Recurring comic function:** The voice of reason in a household of catastrophe. Her competence is so startling against the prevailing chaos that it functions as comedy on its own.

---

### CHARLOTTE LUCAS
**Breed:** Labrador
**Appearance:** Practical dress. Comfortable shoes. The expression of someone who has assessed every available option and made her peace with the results.
**Personality:** Eliza's best friend. Pragmatic to the point of ruthlessness. Marries Collins for the house. Has no regrets. Has considered the alternatives and found them all worse. She is not wrong.
**Recurring comic function:** The ruthless pragmatist. Every romantic illusion Eliza entertains, Charlotte has already stress-tested and discarded. Her comfort in the Collins marriage is funnier the longer it runs.

## 15+ NEW SUPPORTING CHARACTERS

---

### MRS PANDEMONIUM WOOFSLEY
**Breed:** Standard Poodle (with nine miniature Pug companions, all in matching bonnets)
**Appearance:** Enormous, dramatic widow's gown that has been aggressively repurposed for festivity. Hair piled to a dangerous height. Her nine Pugs trail behind her in formation, occasionally breaking formation to chase things.
**Personality:** A scandalous widow who crashes every ball, every dinner, every christening, and at least one funeral she was not invited to. She claims to know everyone. Most people are not sure who she is. She does not care.
**Recurring comic function:** The uninvited chaos injection. Appears at the worst possible moment in any formal event. Her nine Pugs have individual names she announces at volume. Lady W-B considers her a rival. She considers Lady W-B a footnote.

---

### PROFESSOR ALLEGRO BARKSWORTH
**Breed:** Afghan Hound
**Appearance:** Wild hair (which in an Afghan Hound is saying something) flying in all directions. Ancient frock coat covered in ink. Carries a baton at all times and occasionally conducts conversations.
**Personality:** Mary's music teacher. Convinced, against all evidence, that Mary Fetchworth is a prodigy. His conviction is absolute, sincere, and catastrophically wrong. He attends every performance with a look of transported joy that no one else in the room shares.
**Recurring comic function:** The deluded enthusiast. His rapturous response to Mary's playing while the rest of the room clears is a reliable sight gag. He writes letters to conservatories. They do not reply.

---

### THE HUFFINGTON-SCRATCHES
**Breed:** The parents are a Saint Bernard (Sir Reginald) and a Jack Russell Terrier (Lady Moppet). They have seven daughters, all Retrievers of varying colour, all identical in temperament: extremely loud.
**Appearance:** Sir Reginald: enormous, affable, slightly bewildered. Lady Moppet: tiny, ferocious, makes up in volume what she lacks in height. The seven daughters: a blur of golden fur and competing opinions.
**Personality:** The rival family. More daughters, more chaos, louder mother, more dramatic father. Lady Woofington-Barks considers them inferior. Lady Moppet considers Lady Woofington-Barks laughable. Both are correct about the other.
**Recurring comic function:** The Fetchworthsare chaotic. The Huffington-Scratches are chaotic and louder. Every assembly scene involving both families is a volume competition. Sir Reginald and Lord Biscuit-Bottoms have developed a silent bond of mutual survival.

---

### GENERAL SIR HUMPHREY SLOBBERCHOPS
**Breed:** Bullmastiff
**Appearance:** Military uniform loaded with medals, none of which he can satisfactorily account for. Magnificent moustache. Chest out. Enormous hat.
**Personality:** A pompous military general who arrives at Meryton in pursuit of a regiment that left three months ago. Has not been told. Refuses to believe it. Sets up a temporary command post at the coaching inn and issues orders to civilians.
**Recurring comic function:** The authoritative fool. Arrives in every scene expecting deference and receives only polite confusion. His certainty that he is in the right location increases as the evidence against it mounts.

---

### CHEF GASTON FLEABUISSON
**Breed:** French Bulldog
**Appearance:** Toque blanche, food-stained apron, magnificent ears. Gestures with both hands while holding a ladle. Every entrance is an entrance.
**Personality:** Hired to cater Bingley's dinners. Narrates every course in passionate, broken English. Has strong opinions about English cooking (devastating), English manners (barbaric), and English weather (unforgivable). Is somehow the most perceptive character at every dinner party.
**Recurring comic function:** The commentary track. While plating food, he delivers observations on the romantic situation at the table that are completely accurate and entirely ignored because everyone is focused on ignoring each other.

---

### LADY CONSTANCE CRUMBLEWICK
**Breed:** Pembroke Welsh Corgi
**Appearance:** Ancient. Tiny. Wizened. Dressed in black lace from a previous century. Moves on a small wheeled platform pushed by a footman. Eyes that miss absolutely nothing.
**Personality:** Lady Catherine's most feared advisor. Has been at Rosings since the building was new. Nobody knows how old she is. Lady Catherine consults her before every major decision. The advice is always two sentences, always correct, and always slightly frightening.
**Recurring comic function:** The ancient oracle. Her stillness against the room's movement. Her two-line pronouncements that end conversations. The fact that she is approximately the size of a hatbox but commands absolute authority.

---

### THE REVEREND PERCIVAL TAILWAGGER
**Breed:** Old English Sheepdog
**Appearance:** Vicar's vestments slightly too long, trailing behind him. Hair everywhere, covering most of his face. Permanently cheerful expression somewhere behind the fur.
**Personality:** Aggressively, relentlessly cheerful. Has performed every wedding in Meryton for twenty years. Remembers none of them but feels warmly about all of them. Finds every occasion, including funerals, to be an opportunity for a celebration.
**Recurring comic function:** The enthusiastic officiant. Arrives for weddings early, sometimes days early. Blesses things that have not asked to be blessed. His ceremony is fourteen minutes of pure joy and approximately four minutes of legal content.

---

### THE MYSTERIOUS STRANGER: LORD OBSIDIAN VELVETTHORN
**Breed:** Black Norwegian Elkhound
**Appearance:** Arrives mid-series in all black. Hat pulled low. Cloak. The posture of someone with a secret that is structurally load-bearing.
**Personality:** Appears in Episode 211 and refuses to explain himself adequately for approximately thirty episodes. Has some history with Darcy, some history with Wickham, and some interest in Eliza that may or may not be romantic. Leaves as mysteriously as he arrived, having destabilised everything.
**Recurring comic function:** The dramatic pivot. Every scene he enters changes direction. He is played completely straight against the comedy around him, which makes him funnier. His secrets, when revealed, are both shocking and slightly anticlimactic.

---

### MISS HORTENSIA YAPPSWORTH
**Breed:** Yorkshire Terrier
**Appearance:** Tiny. Elaborate ringlets. Dress with too many bows. Carries a very small fan she deploys aggressively.
**Personality:** The county's leading gossip. Not malicious, simply incapable of receiving information without immediately redistributing it. She is the town's news infrastructure. Without her, no one would know anything.
**Recurring comic function:** The information delivery system. Whatever needs the whole village to know, Hortensia knows it first and broadcasts it before the episode ends. Her fan snaps open at every revelation.

---

### SIR BARNABY SNOUTINGTON-CLEVES
**Breed:** Bloodhound
**Appearance:** Enormous drooping face. Magnificent jowls. The expression of someone who has witnessed every form of human folly and is tired. Elegant suit, somehow still dignified.
**Personality:** The county magistrate. Wearily competent. Has dealt with the aftermath of every Fetchworth, Huffington-Scratch, and Woofsley escapade for twenty years. Does his job. Goes home. Stares at a wall.
**Recurring comic function:** The exhausted authority. Every scene he appears in is a scene he would rather not be in. His tolerance for chaos is extraordinary because it has been professionally required.

---

### MRS ARABELLA FLAUNCINGTON
**Breed:** Irish Setter
**Appearance:** Dramatic auburn curls. Gown in deep red. Carries a handkerchief she has never used to wipe a tear, only to wave dramatically for emphasis.
**Personality:** Lady Woofington-Barks's closest friend, fiercest rival, and primary audience. She matches Lady W-B performance for performance. Every Lady W-B crisis is immediately topped by an Arabella crisis from six weeks ago that she has been saving.
**Recurring comic function:** The rival performer. The two of them together create a feedback loop of escalating hysteria. Lord Biscuit-Bottoms and Mr Flauncington have developed identical coping strategies involving newspapers and long walks.

---

### MR FLAUNCINGTON
**Breed:** Irish Terrier
**Appearance:** Quiet. Neat. The look of a man who learned stillness as a survival skill.
**Personality:** Three words maximum per episode. He and Lord Biscuit-Bottoms have an entire friendship conducted in meaningful silences and occasional nods.
**Recurring comic function:** The parallel anchor. His minimal presence next to Arabella's maximum presence mirrors the Biscuit-Bottoms dynamic. The two husbands exchange looks that contain entire novels.

---

### CAPTAIN REGINALD WOOFINGTON IV
**Breed:** Weimaraner
**Appearance:** Naval uniform, braid slightly frayed. Tall. Distracted. Holds a telescope he looks through indoors.
**Personality:** Lady Woofington-Barks's brother. Has been at sea for eleven years and is not entirely sure he has returned. Occasionally gives navigational advice in social situations. His war stories begin in the middle.
**Recurring comic function:** The slightly elsewhere soldier. Arrives for two episodes in season three, contributes a magnificent non-sequitur to the Wickham crisis, and departs again. The narrator describes his return as "technically a visit."

---

### MISS PRUDENCE FETCHWORTH-ADJACENT
**Breed:** Whippet
**Appearance:** Extremely thin. Extremely fast. Dressed in pale grey. Has the energy of someone about to leave a room even when standing still.
**Personality:** A distant Fetchworth cousin nobody invited but who has been present at every family gathering for eight years. Nobody is certain who she is exactly. She has opinions. She shares them at speed.
**Recurring comic function:** The unexplained presence. She is simply there. Background at first, then increasingly prominent. The narrator describes her as "the Fetchworths' least-explained ongoing situation."

---

### LORD PEREGRINE SNIFFLECROFT
**Breed:** Borzoi
**Appearance:** Extremely tall. Extremely thin. Extremely long nose. Peers down it at everything, which given the nose's length is a significant distance.
**Personality:** A foppish aristocratic dog who considers himself above both classes and is tolerated by neither. Owns a monocle and uses it to examine things he has already decided are beneath him.
**Recurring comic function:** The absurd bystander. Wanders into scenes at Meryton, delivers one baffling opinion on the situation, and drifts out. The narrator calls him "everyone's least favourite landmark."

---

### MADAME ZELDA WUFFENBACH
**Breed:** German Shepherd
**Appearance:** Elaborate Central European widow's dress. Many rings. Carries a deck of cards she shuffles constantly. Arrived from somewhere that is never entirely established.
**Personality:** The self-appointed fortune teller of Meryton Assembly. Reads cards, palms, tea leaves, and on one occasion a bowl of soup. Her predictions are alarmingly accurate, which everyone finds unsettling and refuses to acknowledge.
**Recurring comic function:** The accurate oracle nobody listens to. Appears before every major plot development and describes it with eerie precision. Characters dismiss her. The narrator sides with her entirely.

---

### SERGEANT BARKLEY RUFFINGTON
**Breed:** Border Terrier
**Appearance:** Military coat, two sizes large. Enormous whiskers. Marches everywhere, even into furniture.
**Personality:** Wickham's sergeant and accidental conscience. Knows Wickham is lying about everything. Says so repeatedly. Is not believed. Watches events unfold with the face of a man whose warnings have failed.
**Recurring comic function:** The unheeded warning. Every scene he has with Wickham ends with him giving away the plot and being ignored. The narrator describes him as "the only honest soldier in the regiment, which is precisely why no one listens."

# SECTION 3: LOCATION GUIDE

---

## 1. LONGBOURN: THE FETCHWORTH FAMILY HOME

**Visual description for Dreamina:** A cheerful, slightly overcrowded Georgian house, warm candlelight, floral wallpaper in a state of enthusiastic fading, bonnets on every available hook, a front parlour that contains too much furniture and too many daughters simultaneously. Soft morning light in early scenes, warm evening glow in domestic chaos scenes. The garden is visible through large sash windows. Lace curtains, slightly askew. Regency England, provincial warmth, domestic anarchy.

**Who lives here:** Lady Woofington-Barks, Lord Biscuit-Bottoms, Eliza, Jane, Lydia, Kitty, Mary. Occasional visits from Aunt Gardiner, Charlotte Lucas, Mr Collins (unwelcome).

**Comic potential:** Too many characters in too small a space. Every conversation is overheard. Every secret is immediately public. The parlour contains Lady W-B's fainting chaise, the pianoforte of suffering, Lord Biscuit-Bottoms's newspaper fortress, and enough daughters to fill a small parliament. Every meal is an event. Every visitor is a crisis.

---

## 2. NETHERFIELD PARK: BINGLEY'S LEASED ESTATE

**Visual description for Dreamina:** Grand Regency manor, freshly decorated in a style that suggests recent money and excellent taste, pale gold walls, high ceilings, enormous windows overlooking manicured grounds. Chandeliers. Caroline Bingley's touch: everything elegant, slightly cold. Candlelit dining scenes with long tables. Formal drawing rooms. A morning room that catches the light and where Jane is frequently discovered being charming.

**Who lives here (leased):** Lord Bingley Fluffington, Caroline Bingley, Mr and Mrs Hurst (background married couple, she smug, he unconscious in a chair in most scenes).

**Comic potential:** The gap between Caroline's curated elegance and the chaos the Fetchworthsbring with them. Eliza arriving in mud. Lady W-B arriving unannounced. The kitchen scenes with Chef Gaston Fleabuisson delivering commentary. Darcy staring out windows.

---

## 3. PEMBERLEY: DARCY'S ESTATE

**Visual description for Dreamina:** A genuinely magnificent Georgian country house, natural stone, vast grounds, a river valley, proportions that speak of old money and actual taste rather than performance. Interior: rich but understated, dark wood, deep green upholstery, a library that occupies an entire wing. Dramatic natural light, golden hour exterior shots, interior candlelight that is warmer than Netherfield. This house is what Darcy is when nobody is watching him.

**Who lives here:** Lord Darcy Pawsworth, his young sister Miss Georgiana Pawsworth (a shy tortoiseshell cat introduced in Season 3), assorted staff.

**Comic potential:** The revelation that Darcy's home is beautiful and warm is Eliza's turning point and the show's greatest visual joke. The house is exactly what she would choose. She is appalled to find this out. The housekeeper's glowing description of Darcy's character while Eliza tries not to reassess everything.

---

## 4. MERYTON ASSEMBLY ROOMS

**Visual description for Dreamina:** A grand provincial ballroom, chandeliers blazing, Regency dancers in formal dress, card tables to one side, a small orchestra in the gallery, warm golden light on polished floors. The walls are the social event horizon of the county. Everyone important and everyone unimportant arrives here in their best clothes and their worst intentions.

**Who appears here:** The entire cast. This is neutral territory and the series' primary event space.

**Comic potential:** Every social disaster has its premiere in this room. Introductions that go wrong. Dancing that goes wrong. Declarations that go wrong. The orchestra plays on through everything. Madame Zelda Wuffenbach occupies a corner with her cards. Mrs Woofsley and her nine Pugs process through the crowd.

---

## 5. THE GARDEN AT LONGBOURN

**Visual description for Dreamina:** An English cottage garden, overgrown with roses and lavender, gravel paths, a bench under an oak tree, warm summer light, bees, birdsong. Eliza walks here to think. Lady W-B occasionally collapses here more dramatically than indoors allows.

**Who uses it:** Eliza primarily. Jane and Bingley for their first real conversation. Collins's proposal attempt. Darcy's first disastrous almost-confession.

**Comic potential:** The garden is where characters go to be private and are consistently interrupted. Every intimate scene is sabotaged by a Pug, a Collins, or Lady W-B peering through a window with unconcealed interest.

---

## 6. MERYTON HIGH STREET

**Visual description for Dreamina:** A Regency market town high street, cobblestones, shop fronts with bow windows, a milliner, a draper, a circulating library. Morning light. Bustling with bonneted figures. The regiment visible in the distance.

**Who appears here:** Lydia and Kitty (military observation post). Miss Hortensia Yappsworth (intelligence redistribution centre). The regiment including Wickham. Chance encounters designed by the plot.

**Comic potential:** Every piece of news in the county passes through this street. Lydia's pursuit of soldiers. Wickham's performance of honesty. Hortensia's information broadcasting. The street is the series' gossip infrastructure made physical.

---

## 7. COLLINS'S PARSONAGE

**Visual description for Dreamina:** A modest clergyman's house made aggressively tidy by someone who cares too much. Over-polished. Over-decorated with small religious objects and framed certificates. Lady Catherine's estate visible through every window because Collins has arranged his furniture to allow this. A small, airless parlour. Charlotte's personal space: one armchair, one book, a door that closes.

**Who lives here:** Mr Collins, Charlotte Lucas (post-marriage).

**Comic potential:** Charlotte's ruthless contentment versus Collins's obliviousness. Rosings looming through every window. Collins's pride in the parsonage is inversely proportional to its charm. Eliza's visit and the face she does not make.

---

## 8. ROSINGS PARK: LADY CATHERINE'S ESTATE

**Visual description for Dreamina:** An enormous, slightly aggressive Palladian mansion. White stone. Formal gardens in rigid geometric patterns. Interior: gilded, heavy, everything twice the size it needs to be. Portrait of Lady Catherine in every room. Footmen at every corner. The dining room table seats forty and is always fully laid whether guests are present or not. Lady Constance Crumblewick's corner: a high-backed chair in shadows.

**Who lives here:** Lady Catherine de Paw, Lady Constance Crumblewick (ancient Corgi advisor), Miss Anne de Paw (Lady Catherine's silent, pale daughter, a white cat who has never been allowed to form an opinion).

**Comic potential:** Everything is too much. The scale, the gold, the portraits. Every visit by Collins is a religious experience for him and a mild irritation for Lady Catherine, who nonetheless depends on his worship. Eliza's visit where she refuses to be intimidated.

---

## 9. THE COACHING INN: THE SPOTTED HOUND

**Visual description for Dreamina:** A warm, slightly smoky coaching inn interior, low beams, firelight, a landlord behind a bar polishing things, travellers at long wooden tables, the smell of horse and hay implied by the visuals. Ground floor: public bar, chaos. Private dining room upstairs: marginally less chaos. General Slobberchops has occupied a corner table and declared it a command post.

**Who appears here:** Wickham (frequently). The regiment (enthusiastically). Lydia (she should not be here). General Slobberchops (permanently). Passing strangers with information.

**Comic potential:** The one location where class distinctions blur. Aristocrats in transit, soldiers on leave, merchants and Fetchworth daughters all collide here. Wickham does most of his damage in this room.

## 10. THE WUFFENBACH FORTUNE TENT: MERYTON FAIR

**Visual description for Dreamina:** A brightly coloured tent at the edge of the Meryton fair, interior hung with silks in deep purple and gold, a single candle, a round table with cards laid out, smoke implied by the lighting. Outside: the fair in full Regency chaos, bunting, livestock, competing vendors, the Huffington-Scratch daughters at volume.

# SECTION 4: MUSIC CUE GUIDE

---

## 1. THE BISCUIT-BOTTOMS DEADPAN
**Style:** Sparse, dry harpsichord. Single notes. Long pauses. Chamber music that has given up.
**When to use:** Whenever Lord Biscuit-Bottoms delivers a one-liner or raises the newspaper.
**Example moment:** Lady W-B announces they are all ruined. Long pause. Harpsichord note. Biscuit-Bottoms turns a page.

---

## 2. LADY WOOFINGTON-BARKS OVERTURE
**Style:** Frantic string quartet, rising tempo, slightly out of control. As if a Haydn symphony had a panic attack.
**When to use:** Lady W-B enters a room with news, or news enters a room and finds Lady W-B.
**Example moment:** The letter announcing Bingley's arrival at Netherfield. Lady W-B reads it. The strings start. She turns to the household.

---

## 3. THE DARCY STARE
**Style:** A single cello note, sustained, slightly ominous, then resolving into something that might be longing.
**When to use:** Any sustained eye contact between Darcy and Eliza.
**Example moment:** Darcy watches Eliza across the Meryton ballroom. The cello holds. He does not look away.

---

## 4. FULL REGENCY WALTZ
**Style:** Bright, sweeping orchestral waltz, full strings and winds, formally correct, genuinely beautiful.
**When to use:** Any actual dancing sequence at the Assembly or Netherfield.
**Example moment:** Darcy asks Eliza to dance. Both are furious about it. The waltz plays on, perfectly lovely.

---

## 5. WICKHAM THEME
**Style:** Smooth, charming woodwind melody, clarinet-led, pleasant surface, slightly too perfect. A tune that smiles too wide.
**When to use:** Wickham enters or begins one of his performances of honesty.
**Example moment:** Wickham meets Eliza on Meryton High Street and begins explaining his history with Darcy. The clarinet purrs.

---

## 6. THE LYDIA GALLOP
**Style:** Fast, percussive, slightly unhinged polka. Fiddle-forward. The musical equivalent of a spotted dog running toward something she should not.
**When to use:** Lydia enters, Lydia schemes, Lydia drags Kitty somewhere inadvisable.
**Example moment:** Lydia spots soldiers on the High Street. The polka fires up. Kitty is already being pulled along.

---

## 7. THE CAROLINE SMILE
**Style:** Elegant, cool string quartet, perfectly composed, with one slightly sour note held beneath the beauty.
**When to use:** Caroline delivers a compliment that is actually an insult. Caroline enters a room Eliza is in.
**Example moment:** Caroline tells Eliza her dress is charming, in a tone that suggests the opposite. The strings are immaculate.

---

## 8. PEMBERLEY REVEAL
**Style:** Swelling, genuinely gorgeous full orchestra, the kind of music that makes a room feel larger. Major key, warm, unhurried.
**When to use:** First sight of Pemberley. Eliza's moment of dawning reassessment.
**Example moment:** Eliza and the Gardiners crest the hill. Pemberley is below. The orchestra opens up. Eliza's expression shifts.

---

## 9. THE COLLINS MARCH
**Style:** Pompous, slightly flat brass march. The musical equivalent of someone who has ironed his socks. Self-important 4/4 time.
**When to use:** Collins arrives. Collins proposes. Collins announces anything.
**Example moment:** Collins straightens his oversized waistcoat and begins his proposal to Eliza. The brass announces his intention.

---

## 10. MARY'S PIANOFORTE OF SUFFERING
**Style:** Technically correct but rhythmically uncertain pianoforte. Chopin played as if the pianist has read about Chopin but not heard him.
**When to use:** Mary performs. The room empties. Mary continues.
**Example moment:** Mary begins her after-dinner piece. The room begins to thin. Mary plays on. Professor Barksworth weeps with joy.

---

## 11. THE SCANDAL STING
**Style:** Short, sharp string pizzicato. Three notes. Boom. Pause. Use sparingly.
**When to use:** The moment a secret is revealed, a letter is opened, or a lie is exposed.
**Example moment:** Colonel Fitzwilliam accidentally tells Eliza that Darcy separated Jane and Bingley. Three pizzicato notes. Eliza's face.

---

## 12. THE FETCHWORTH FAMILY THEME
**Style:** Warm, slightly chaotic chamber music, oboe and strings, major key with occasional comic wrong notes. Affectionate and mildly unhinged.
**When to use:** Establishing shots of Longbourn, family meals, the household in full domestic chaos.
**Example moment:** Morning at Longbourn. All five daughters and both parents descend to breakfast simultaneously. Theme plays.

---

## 13. THE REGIMENT FANFARE
**Style:** Military brass, drums, upbeat, designed to sound impressive and be mildly ridiculous in context.
**When to use:** The regiment arrives in Meryton. Soldiers march. Lydia and Kitty watch from the High Street.
**Example moment:** The regiment processes through Meryton. The fanfare plays. Lydia's ears go up.

---

## 14. THE NARRATOR'S GRACE NOTE
**Style:** A single, elegant harpsichord arpeggio, ascending. Brief. Decorative. Slightly smug.
**When to use:** At the moment the narrator delivers a particularly devastating observation.
**Example moment:** The narrator describes Lady W-B's scheme as "the most strategically chaotic social manoeuvre since the last one." The arpeggio. The narrator continues.

---

## 15. THE ROSINGS THEME
**Style:** Enormous, overblown baroque organ chords, as if a cathedral were being played at the wrong speed. Gilded and slightly oppressive.
**When to use:** Establishing shots of Rosings. Lady Catherine's entrances. Any scene where Lady Catherine pronounces judgment.
**Example moment:** Lady Catherine raises her lorgnette. The organ sounds. She begins to speak.

---

## 16. THE LOVE THEME (ELIZA AND DARCY)
**Style:** Tender, unhurried strings, piano leading, the kind of melody that takes its time because it knows it will arrive. Develops across the series from fragment to full statement.
**When to use:** Moments of genuine connection between Eliza and Darcy. Rare. Significant.
**Example moment:** Darcy hands Eliza his letter. She takes it. A single phrase of the love theme. Just the opening. Not yet.

---

## 17. GASTON'S DINNER COMMENTARY
**Style:** Lively French cafe music, accordion, light and fast. Completely out of period, completely correct for the character.
**When to use:** Chef Gaston Fleabuisson serves a course and delivers his observations on the dining situation.
**Example moment:** Gaston places the soup and murmurs that the young lady in the blue dress is making eyes at the gentleman who is pretending not to notice. The accordion agrees.

---

## 18. THE ELOPEMENT CHASE
**Style:** Fast, panicked strings, escalating tempo, the musical equivalent of a chase scene involving four carriages and a mule.
**When to use:** The Lydia-Wickham elopement crisis. Any scene involving running after a carriage.
**Example moment:** The note is discovered. Lydia is gone. The strings begin. Lord Biscuit-Bottoms looks up from his newspaper.

---

## 19. THE RESOLUTION THEME
**Style:** Full, warm, unhurried orchestral conclusion. All instruments. Major key. The sound of things going right after a long time of going wrong.
**When to use:** The second proposal scene. The wedding. The epilogue.
**Example moment:** Darcy proposes again, correctly this time. Eliza says yes. The resolution theme begins its full statement for the first time in 367 episodes.

---

## 20. LADY W-B'S TRIUMPH
**Style:** Extravagant, operatic strings with trumpet flourish. The musical equivalent of a curtain call.
**When to use:** Lady W-B's moments of validated scheming. The double engagement announcement. Her speeches about matrimony.
**Example moment:** Bingley proposes to Jane. Lady W-B hears the news. The triumph theme fires up. She turns to Lord Biscuit-Bottoms. He turns a page.

# SECTION 5: PRODUCTION RULES

---

## FORMAT AND FRAMING

Every episode is exactly 15 seconds. No exceptions. No credits over content. Opening: one establishing shot (0.5 to 1 second). Middle: one to two action beats (8 to 12 seconds). Close: one reaction or punchline shot (2 to 3 seconds). Vertical format: 9:16 aspect ratio for all primary output. Composition for vertical: characters framed from the waist up or in three-quarter shot. Physical comedy requires full body framing, pull out to accommodate.

All Dreamina image generation settings: Regency England period accuracy, bipedal anthropomorphic animals, consistent character design across all episodes, warm painterly style, controlled lighting per location (see Location Guide), no modern elements, no contemporary clothing references.

**NO TEXT ON SCREEN. EVER.** Every Dreamina prompt must include: "NO text, NO subtitles, NO captions, NO words, NO letters, NO titles on screen anywhere." Dialogue is described as character action and performance only. It is never requested as on-screen text. This is an absolute rule with zero exceptions.

---

## TELEGRAM DELIVERY METHOD

Episodes are numbered and titled before production begins (see Section 6). Each episode brief delivered as: episode number, title, logline, shot list (two to four shots described for Dreamina), music cue reference number, narrator VO text. Shot descriptions must include: character, location, action, expression, clothing detail for character consistency. VO text must be written before shot generation begins.

---

## CHARACTER CONSISTENCY RULES

Each character has a fixed visual signature that never varies: Lady W-B always has bonnet, always pink gown, always pearls clutched. Lord Biscuit-Bottoms always has sliding wig, velvet waistcoat, broadsheet newspaper. Darcy always has one eyebrow raised, navy tailcoat, expression suggesting mild disdain. Wickham always has the 10% too-wide smile. Caroline always has the fixed, empty smile. These are non-negotiable. Character breeds are non-negotiable. Bipedal upright posture at all times, no exceptions. No character appears without their visual signature intact.

---

## VO USAGE RULES

The narrator speaks in every episode. No episode is narration-free. VO is recorded separately and laid over visuals. VO maximum: three sentences per episode. VO is always third person. VO never quotes characters directly. VO delivers: context, irony, gossip, foreshadowing, judgment. VO never over-explains the visual joke. If the image is funny, the VO comments sideways. If the image needs context, VO provides it briskly and moves on. The narrator has a personality: she is dry, posh, faintly delighted by suffering, and always two steps ahead. She is never shocked. She already knew.

---

## CLASSICAL MUSIC MANDATE

Every episode uses a named cue from the Music Cue Guide (Section 4). No episode uses unlicensed contemporary music. All music is classical period-appropriate or in classical style. Music cues must be matched to scene content per the guide: Biscuit-Bottoms deadpan gets the harpsichord, Wickham gets the clarinet theme, Pemberley gets the full orchestra swell. Multiple cues may appear in one episode if episode content warrants a tonal shift. The music director receives the music cue reference number with each episode brief.
